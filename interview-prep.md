# Workflow: Interview Prep & Opportunity Assessment

## Purpose
Preparing for interviews and assessing new opportunities are among my highest-value AI use cases. This workflow ensures I walk into every conversation well-prepared and make clear-headed decisions about which opportunities to pursue.

---

## Phase 1 — Opportunity Assessment

When a new role or opportunity arrives:

**Prompt:**
> "Read `me/cv.md`, `career/target-roles.md`, and `career/status.md`. Then assess this opportunity: [paste JD or description]. Score it against my must-haves first. Identify the top three reasons this could be right, and the top three concerns or gaps. What questions should I be asking before I go further?"

Output this into a new file: `career/opportunities/[opportunity-name].md` using `templates/opportunity-assessment.md`.

---

## Phase 2 — Interview Preparation

Once I decide to proceed:

**Prompt:**
> "I have an interview with [organisation type / role] at [date/time]. Read `me/cv.md`, `me/identity.md`, and `career/opportunities/[opportunity-name].md`. Prepare me for this interview:
> 1. The five most likely questions and strong answers drawing on my actual experience
> 2. Three questions I should ask them that demonstrate strategic thinking
> 3. Any gaps between my experience and the role that I should be ready to address
> 4. The two or three things I most want them to remember about me"

---

## Phase 3 — Post-Interview Debrief

After the interview, drop notes into `inbox/` and run:

> "Process my post-interview notes from [filename]. Update `career/opportunities/[opportunity-name].md` with: how it went, what I picked up about the organisation, outstanding questions, and my honest read on fit and likelihood."

---

## My interview style

- Direct and confident — I don't undersell, but I don't over-pitch either
- Specific and evidenced — I answer with real examples, not generalities
- Curious — I ask genuine questions, not just impressive-sounding ones
- Honest about what I want — I don't pretend a role is perfect if it isn't

AI should prepare me to be more of myself, not to perform a character.
