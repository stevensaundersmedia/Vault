# Workflow: Presentation Building

## Purpose
PowerPoint is a time-sink for me. This workflow uses AI to do the structural and content heavy lifting so I can focus on the message and the final edit.

---

## My honest relationship with presentations

I am not a natural slide-builder. I think in paragraphs, not bullets. My instinct is to over-write slides and under-design them. AI helps me flip this — get the structure right, keep the slides lean, make sure each one earns its place.

---

## Step 1 — Brief

Before starting any presentation, answer these questions (paste into AI):

1. **Who is the audience?** (seniority, sector, prior knowledge of the topic)
2. **What is the single thing they should remember?** (the one-line takeaway)
3. **What do I want them to do or feel after this?** (action, agreement, enthusiasm, trust)
4. **What is the context?** (pitch, update, keynote, workshop, proposal)
5. **How long do I have?** (in minutes — AI will estimate slide count)
6. **What do I already have?** (notes, documents, data I want to draw from)

---

## Step 2 — Structure

**Prompt:**
> "I'm building a presentation. Here is my brief: [paste brief above]. Propose a narrative arc — the sequence of ideas from opening to close. Give me the structure as a list of section headings with a one-sentence description of what each section does. No slides yet — just the skeleton."

Review and agree the structure before moving to slides.

---

## Step 3 — Slide outlines

**Prompt:**
> "Using this structure [paste agreed structure], generate the slide-by-slide outline. For each slide give me: the headline (one strong thought, not a label), the key content (3 bullets maximum per slide), and any visual suggestion. Keep headlines punchy — they should be the argument, not the topic."

---

## Step 4 — Speaker notes

**Prompt:**
> "Add speaker notes to each slide. Write them in my voice per `me/voice.md` — these are what I will actually say, not what's on the slide. Each slide note should be 3–5 sentences that expand and personalise the point."

---

## Slide writing principles

- Headlines are thoughts, not topics ("We missed our window" beats "Q3 Performance")
- Maximum 3 bullets per slide — usually fewer
- One idea per slide
- The opening slide should create tension or curiosity, not just announce the topic
- The closing slide should be the most memorable thing in the deck

---

## Handing off to design

Once structure and content are approved, I take the outline to slides. Ask AI to format the final content list cleanly for handoff to a designer or for direct entry into PowerPoint / Keynote / Google Slides.
