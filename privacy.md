# Privacy Rules

These rules apply in every AI session. They are non-negotiable. If a task requires crossing one of these lines, flag it and stop.

---

## Hard limits

### 1. No financial details
Do not store, surface, or reference specific financial information — account details, salary figures, net worth, debt levels, or financial positions of any kind.

**Exception:** If I provide specific numbers in a session for a calculation (e.g. comparing savings rates, modelling an option), use them for that task only. Do not carry them forward or write them into vault files.

### 2. No family names
Refer to my family only as:
- **My wife**
- **My eldest**
- **My youngest**

Never attempt to infer, remember, or use their actual names, even if I have mentioned them casually in session.

### 3. No confidential employer information
Do not include, store, or reference:
- My current employer's client names or relationships
- Internal strategy, commercial terms, or operational detail
- Anything that would be considered confidential under a standard employment contract

My current employer is referred to generically as "my current employer" in the vault. No further detail.

### 4. No health information
Do not store or reference medical history, diagnoses, prescriptions, or health conditions of any kind.

**What is permitted:**
- Workout data and training stats
- Bioage tracking figures I explicitly provide
- General fitness protocols and performance metrics

---

## Tiered sensitivity

| Type of information | AI tools | Vault storage |
|---|---|---|
| CV and career history | Yes | Yes |
| Writing samples | Yes | Yes |
| Project context (non-confidential) | Yes | Yes |
| Network contacts (first name + context only) | Yes, in session | Yes, with care |
| Workout / fitness stats | Yes | Yes |
| Financial calculations | In session only | Never |
| Family members' identities | Never | Never |
| Employer confidential info | Never | Never |
| Health information | Never | Never |

---

## If in doubt

Ask before proceeding. A brief pause is always preferable to a privacy error.
