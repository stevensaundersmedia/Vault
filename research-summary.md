# Workflow: Research Summarisation

## Purpose
When I need to get up to speed quickly on a topic — a sector, an organisation, a technology, a competitor — I use AI to compress research into something I can act on.

---

## How I want research summarised

Not: a list of bullet points that reads like a Wikipedia article.

Yes: a tight, intelligent summary that tells me what matters, what is contested, what I should be asking, and what would change my thinking.

---

## Standard research prompt

> "Research [topic]. Give me:
> 1. The essential context in 3–4 sentences — what I need to know to have an informed conversation about this
> 2. The key dynamics or tensions right now — what is shifting or being debated
> 3. Two or three things most people get wrong or oversimplify about this
> 4. What a sharp person in [my sector] would want to know that a generic overview wouldn't tell them
> 5. Three questions I should be asking that would demonstrate genuine expertise"

---

## Organisation / competitor research prompt

> "Research [organisation name]. I am preparing for [context — meeting, pitch, interview]. Give me:
> 1. What they do and who they serve — in plain terms
> 2. Their strategic priorities right now based on public information
> 3. Any notable recent moves, partnerships, or announcements
> 4. What challenges they are likely facing
> 5. What I should know about their culture and decision-making style if available
> 6. Three smart questions I could ask in a conversation with someone senior there"

---

## Conference / sector prep prompt

> "I'm attending [event/conference] in [sector]. Help me prepare:
> 1. The key talking points and debates in the sector right now
> 2. Who the notable organisations and figures are that I'm likely to encounter
> 3. What questions and themes will dominate conversations
> 4. What perspective I might usefully bring from my background in [area]"
