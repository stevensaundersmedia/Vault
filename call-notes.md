# Workflow: Call Notes & Follow-Up

## Purpose
I write extensive notes after calls and meetings. This workflow turns raw notes into structured records and drives proactive follow-up — deadlines, actions, next conversations.

---

## Step 1 — Capture

Drop raw notes into `inbox/` immediately after the call. Name the file:
`YYYY-MM-DD-[person or meeting name].md`

Don't worry about structure at capture time. Speed matters more.

---

## Step 2 — AI processing prompt

Once notes are in the inbox, use this prompt:

> "Read my notes from [filename] in the inbox. Process them into a structured call note using the template at `templates/call-note.md`. Extract all action items, flag any deadlines, and identify the next logical follow-up action and a suggested timeline for it."

---

## Step 3 — File the processed note

Processed notes go to:
- `career/network/[person].md` — if it's a networking conversation, update or create the contact file
- `career/opportunities/[opportunity].md` — if it relates to a specific role or pitch
- `projects/[project].md` — if it relates to a live project

---

## Step 4 — Follow-up tracking

Each processed note should produce:
- A list of **my actions** with suggested deadlines
- A list of **their actions** I'm waiting on, with a chase date
- A suggested **next conversation** hook — what to follow up on and why

Ask AI to flag these when processing. Add them to your weekly schedule file.

---

## My note-taking style
I write extensively and in full sentences. My notes often contain analysis and reaction alongside factual record. AI should preserve the intelligence in the notes, not flatten them into bullet points.
